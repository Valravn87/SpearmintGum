1893

Wrigley's Spearmint was launched as the company's second oldest brand, after Juicy Fruit.


1924

Ernest Hare & Billy Jones released the song "Does The Spearmint Lose its Flavor On The Bedpost Overnight?", thereby cementing the brand in the public consciousness.


1944

As the Second World War raged on, company president Phillip Wrigley removed Wrigley's Spearmint, Doublemint, and Juicy Fruit from the civilian market. Wartime rationing meant that Wrigley could not supply enough gum for the entire country, so their entire output of these brands was restricted to the U.S. Armed Forces


1946

Wrigley's Spearmint was reintroduced to the civilian market.


2004

Wrigley's Spearmint was relaunched in the US and UK bearing the slogan of "even better, longer lasting."


2008

Wrigley's launched the Slim Pack.


2009

The launch of the Slim Pack 3 Pack followed shortly after the standard Slim Pack.


2013

The song "Does The Spearmint..." was featured in an episode of the fourth season of Boardwalk Empire.
